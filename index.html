Tell me about Black Wall Street
Black Wall Street was the Greenwood District of Tulsa, Oklahoma — a thriving Black business hub in the early 1900s. Home to over 300 Black-owned businesses including banks, hotels, and law offices, it represented remarkable economic self-sufficiency.

In May–June 1921, it was destroyed in one of America's worst acts of racial violence — the Tulsa Race Massacre. Over 35 blocks were burned, 10,000 were left homeless, and hundreds were killed.
What happened to the survivors?
Despite devastation, the community rebuilt. Many survivors returned and reconstructed their businesses. Legal reparations efforts continue today, with the 2021 centennial bringing renewed national attention…

About Us

Why Blackipedia Matters

For too long, Black history has been footnoted, erased, or distorted. Blackipedia exists to reclaim, preserve, and amplify the full breadth of the African diaspora's contributions to human civilization.

We believe knowledge is power. By unifying Black stories from every corner of the globe into one accessible, AI-enhanced platform, we empower individuals and communities to educate themselves and future generations.
